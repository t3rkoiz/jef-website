Our History

The Jamaica Employers' Federation (JEF) was established in 1958 with the purpose of representing and safeguarding the interests of employers in their relationship with government and organised labour. Its mission is to lead the development of an enabling business environment through effective representation and the provision of value-added services that strengthen the ability of employers to optimize competitiveness and workplace harmony.

As the only organization of its kind in Jamaica, JEF has a growing membership of over 300 corporate and individual members. JEF is also a member of the International Organization of Employers (IOE) and the Caribbean Employers' Confederation (CEC). JEF represents Jamaica's sole employer representative to the International Labour Organization (ILO), which is the only UN body with a tripartite structure that facilitates the interests of workers, governments, and employers.

Through extensive research and developmental activities, as well as its international affiliations, JEF has the expertise and reach to successfully fulfil its mandate both locally and regionally. The organization has significantly expanded its range of services to better support the business development and productivity needs of Jamaican employers.

JEF's suite of services includes IR Consultancy, Research & Development, Membership Services, Events Planning & Coordination, Occupational Safety & Health, Learning, Human Resources Development & Workplace Solutions. With these services, JEF is well-equipped to provide its members with the necessary tools and resources to succeed in today's dynamic business environment.

Leadership Voices

Messages from Leadership

Wayne Chen

President, Jamaica Employers' Federation

JEF is the longstanding voice of the country's employers and a key tripartite constituent with the Government of Jamaica and Jamaica Confederation of Trade Unions (JCTU). We advocate for priority areas that emphasize business enablement and success, workforce competitiveness, and sustainable labour market practices. Our key focus areas include: improving productivity through upskilling and technology adoption; digital transformation integrating AI and automation; addressing skills gaps through targeted initiatives; labour market reform; and promoting job creation with a just transition to green jobs.

O'Neil W. Grant, OD, MBA

Chief Executive Officer

As we formulate plans for the 2024–2029 strategic period, we must assess our position, make necessary adjustments, and set targets to achieve our programs and initiatives. We are reshaping and recommitting to our vision, mission, and core values, keeping our members at the heart of our operations. The Jamaica Employers' Federation will strengthen institutional capacities, increase visibility through PR and communication, and bring back staples such as the face-to-face Convention, CEO Breakfast, and member sensitization fora. The future for JEF is quite interesting, and our strategic plan will lead us in the right direction.
Global Partnerships

Our Affiliations

International Organisation of Employers (IOE)

A founding member of the IOE, JEF participates in global employer advocacy and representation on the world stage.

Caribbean Employers' Confederation (CEC)

An active member of CEC, which now spans 19 countries in the Caribbean region. JEF's President serves as Immediate Past President of the CEC.

International Labour Organization (ILO)

JEF is the sole employer representative to the ILO in Jamaica and the only organisation of its kind in the region representing employers' interests.
